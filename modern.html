Introduction

The modern Indian military has evolved significantly since India's independence in 1947. The Indian Armed Forces consist of the Army, Navy, and Air Force, each playing a crucial role in national defense and security. This section covers the major conflicts, strategic developments, and advancements in military technology that have shaped the modern Indian military.

Major Conflicts and Operations

Indo-Pakistani Wars

The Indo-Pakistani Wars are a series of conflicts fought between India and Pakistan, including the wars of 1947-1948, 1965, 1971, and the Kargil War in 1999. Each conflict had significant impacts on the geopolitical landscape of South Asia.

Sino-Indian War (1962)

The Sino-Indian War of 1962 was a border conflict between India and China, primarily over disputed territories. The war highlighted the need for modernization and strategic reforms in the Indian military.

Operation Blue Star

Operation Blue Star was a military operation carried out in 1984 to remove armed militants from the Golden Temple in Amritsar. It was a significant and controversial operation in India's military history.

Modernization and Technological Advancements

Missile Development

India has made significant advancements in missile technology, including the development of ballistic missiles like Agni and Prithvi, and cruise missiles like BrahMos. These developments have enhanced India's strategic capabilities.

Indigenous Defense Production

India has focused on indigenizing its defense production through initiatives like the Make in India program. Key projects include the development of the Tejas fighter aircraft, Arjun tank, and various naval vessels.

Space and Cyber Capabilities

India has also enhanced its space and cyber capabilities, with significant achievements like the anti-satellite (ASAT) missile test and advancements in cybersecurity infrastructure to protect against modern threats.

Strategic Partnerships and Alliances

India has developed strategic partnerships and alliances with various countries to enhance its defense capabilities and ensure regional stability. Key partnerships include those with the United States, Russia, Israel, and France.
